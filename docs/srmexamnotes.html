Question 1

Suppose you have estimated the following regression model on a representative sample of the UK population \[\log(Weight_i)=\beta_0+\beta_1 Female_i+\beta_2 AGE_i +\beta_3+AGE_i^2+\epsilon_i\]

where Weight_i is weight of a person in 100s of kilograms Suppose you find that \(\hat\beta_1=-0.10\) Which of the following is a correct interpretation?

  1. The average weight for women is 10kg
  2. Women on average - weigh 10 kg less than men
  3. Women on average weigh 10% less than men
  4. None of the above.

Correct answer


Question 2

For the model from Question 1: suppose you find that \(\hat\beta_2=0.01\) and \(\hat\beta_3=-0.0001\) . What does it tell you about the age at which we expect people to be heaviest?

  1. At 25 years
  2. At 75 years
  3. At 40 years
  4. None of the above

Correct answer

Because \(\beta_3\) is negative the weight-age relationship is hump shaped. We can find the maximum of the hump by setting the gradient equal to 0 implying

\[0.01-2\times 0.0001\times AGE=0\] Consequently:

\[AGE=\frac{0.01}{2\times 0.0001}=50\]
